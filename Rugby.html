Rugby History

Rugby is a sport that originated in England in the early 19th century. It is believed to have been created by a student named William Webb Ellis, who, during a game of football, picked up the ball and ran with it. This act of defiance against the rules of football is said to have given birth to rugby.

Rugby quickly gained popularity and spread to other countries, particularly in the British Empire. The sport evolved over time, with different variations emerging, such as rugby union and rugby league. Rugby union is the more traditional form of the game, while rugby league is a faster-paced version that is popular in certain regions.

Today, rugby is played at both amateur and professional levels around the world. It is particularly popular in countries such as New Zealand, South Africa, Australia, England, and France. The sport has a rich history and continues to be a beloved pastime for millions of fans worldwide.

Rugby Rules

  • Rugby is played with two teams of 15 players each.
  • The objective is to score points by carrying, passing or kicking the ball to the opponent's end zone.
  • Players can only pass the ball backward, not forward.
  • Tackling is allowed, but players must use their arms and not their legs.
  • The game is divided into two halves, each lasting 40 minutes.
