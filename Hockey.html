Hockey History

Hockey has a rich history, with the sport evolving over the centuries. The game is believed to have originated in England in the 19th century and it has since become popular in many countries, particularly in the Commonwealth nations.

Hockey is played on a field with a ball and sticks, and it is known for its fast-paced action and physicality. The sport has produced many legendary players and memorable moments, making it a beloved game for fans around the world.

Today, hockey continues to grow in popularity, with professional leagues and international competitions showcasing the best talent in the sport. Whether you're a player, coach or fan, hockey offers an exciting and dynamic experience that brings people together through their love of the game.

Hockey Rules

  • Hockey is played with two teams of 6 players each.
  • The objective is to score points by shooting the puck into the opponent's goal.
  • Players can only move the puck using their sticks, not their hands.
  • Physical contact is allowed, but players must follow specific rules to avoid penalties.
  • The game is divided into two halves, each lasting 40 minutes.
