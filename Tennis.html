Tennis Origins

Tennis is believed to have originated in France in the 12th century, where it was played as a handball game called "jeu de paume." The game evolved over time, with players using rackets to hit the ball instead of their hands. Tennis gained popularity in England during the 16th century and eventually spread worldwide, becoming one of the most popular sports globally.

Today, tennis is played at both amateur and professional levels, with major tournaments such as Wimbledon, the US Open, the French Open, and the Australian Open attracting top players from around the world. The sport continues to evolve, with advancements in technology and training methods contributing to the development of players and the overall growth of tennis as a global sport.

Tennis Rules

  • Tennis is played with two players (singles) or four players (doubles).
  • The objective is to hit the ball over the net and make it land within the opponent's side of the court.
  • Players can hit the ball with their racket, but cannot use their hands (except for the server).
  • The game is divided into sets, each consisting of games, and the first player to win a specified number of sets wins the match.
