Soccer History

Soccer, also known as football outside the United States and Canada, is one of the world's most popular sports. The game has a rich history that spans centuries, with its origins tracing back to ancient civilizations.

The modern form of soccer as we know it today began to take shape in the 19th century in England. The first official rules of the game were established in 1863 when the Football Association (FA) was founded. This marked the beginning of organized soccer, and the sport quickly gained popularity across the globe.

Over the years, soccer has evolved and grown in popularity, with the establishment of various leagues, tournaments and international competitions. The FIFA World Cup, held every four years, is the most prestigious tournament in soccer and attracts millions of viewers worldwide.

Today, soccer is played by millions of people of all ages and skill levels around the world. It is a sport that brings people together, promotes teamwork and sportsmanship, and continues to inspire passion and excitement among fans and players alike.

Soccer Rules

  • Soccer is played with two teams of 11 players each.
  • The objective is to score goals by kicking the ball into the opponent's goal.
  • Players can pass the ball forward, but cannot use their hands (except for the goalkeeper within their penalty area).
  • The game is divided into two halves, each lasting 45 minutes.
